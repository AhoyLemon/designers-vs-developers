What we want to avoid...

There's one thing that we want to avoid and that is...

A bad relationship.

Bad collaboration makes for a bad relationship.

Bad relationships make bad websites.

But also, they make you feel bad.

In a bad relationship:

Designer unnecessarily restricted
Developer disrespected
Anyone unheard
  • Designer unable to diverge from restrictive, boring template.
  • Developer is a set of hands.
  • Both parties can't communicate what they think or want.

Instead we want...

In a good relationship:

  • Ask questions
  • Suggest improvements
  • Respect the others' craft
  • Say it, hear it, get over it!

Kurt Russell & Goldie Hawn (together since 1983, never married)

  • You should feel comfortable asking questions of each other, dig into why certain things are the way they are.
  • You should want to make the project better. What they do, you want to implement & improve upon.
  • And thru it all you should do this while respecting what the other person does.
  • Say the thing, but....
  • Once you've said it, respect the expert of that domain.

The THIRD most important thing I want to tell you:

When we've heard you, we've heard you. We will consider it, and that's it.

Part of respect means believing you can make your case once.

You saying the thing out loud does not mean we need to do it your way.

Nice Things

So now that we've looked at relationship, let's talk about nice things.

Here's some nice things...

  • Sketch & share concept or prototype
  • Share a work in progress
  • Invite to quick huddle
  • Compliments in general
  1. A sketch can help a lot. If you have a rough idea, that could be used for more work later on.
  2. Seeing a work in progress is nice. It involves others into the process and gives early indicators of trajectory.
  3. If someone has expertise you could use, they'd likely be happy to share that if you're respectful of their time.
  4. And also be nice.

ON NICE:Clients will take every opportunity to pick apart a piece of work. They won't spend as much time appreciating stuff. Fill that gap.
